Why Water?

Water scarcity and drought are issues that already affect billions of people and will only be exacerbated by climate change. Even more so, the most vulnerable populations are the ones who are most at risk of losing access to clean drinking water. It is imperative to solve the water scarcity crisis as this precious resource becomes scarcer.

  • An estimated 2.4 billion people lived in water-stressed countries in 2020, of which almost 800 million lived in high and critically high water-stressed countries.

  • Around 66% of the global population lives in a water basin that encounters water stress for at least part of the year.

  • 85% of people affected by droughts live in low- or middle-income countries.

Water Pollution

  • Water Pollution

    • Organic Water Pollution

      • Organic water pollution involves natural or biodegradable substances from living organisms, like sewage or decaying organic matter, often depleting oxygen and fostering harmful algal blooms.

    • Chemical Water Pollution

      • Chemical water pollution results from synthetic or inorganic substances, from sources like industry or agriculture, posing risks to aquatic life and human health. This is mostly done by humans by the plastic and the chemicals thrown in the rivers.

Solutions

Slide 1: Maintain Cleanliness

If you can, pick up litter and garbage by hand, and take it to your personal garbage can before it travels into local waterways. Water cleanliness mandates efficient waste management, industry regulation, and sustainable farming to protect aquatic ecosystems.

Slide 2: Green agriculture

Globally, agriculture accounts for 70% of water resources, so it is essential to have climate-friendly crops, efficient irrigation that reduces the need for water and energy-efficient food production. Green agriculture is also crucial to limit the chemicals that enter the water.

Slide 3: Plastic Waste Reduction

Plastic waste reduction aims to minimize the use of single-use plastics, promote recycling, and adopt eco-friendly alternatives. It include public awareness campaigns, policy interventions, and industry innovations to mitigate plastic pollution's environmental impact and safeguard marine ecosystems.

Slide 4: Wastewater treatment

Wastewater treatment involves processes to remove contaminants from water before usage in environment. Initially, physical methods like screening and sedimentation remove large particles. Then, biological processes use microorganisms to break down organic matter. Finally, chemical treatments disinfect the water.

Drought

What is Drought?

Drought is a complex phenomenon which is difficult to monitor. Drought impacts many sectors of the economy and it occurs on different time scales.
The 4 types of droughts and its causes are listed below :

  • Meteorological Drought - Occurs due to persisting dry weather in an area.
  • Hydrological Drought - It happens due to low water availability in reservoirs, streams, and depleted groundwater levels. This type of drought happens after many months of meteorological drought.
  • Agricultural Drought - It occurs when crops are affected by droughts.
  • Socio economic drought - It occurs when supply and demand are affected by droughts.

How can it be Prevented?

Some of the measures that can be taken to control or solve the problem of drought are listed below :

  • Practice Rainwater harvesting
  • Removal of dissolved miniral salts in water
  • Use Drip Irrigation
  • Plant Maximum Trees
  • Recycle organic waste
